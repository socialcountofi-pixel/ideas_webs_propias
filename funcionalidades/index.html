Introducción

Los condicionales expresan relaciones entre una condición y una consecuencia. En inglés hay varias estructuras según la probabilidad y el tiempo (presente, pasado, posibilidad real o hipotética).

Zero conditional (verdades generales)

Uso: hechos generales, leyes de la naturaleza, rutinas. La condición y la consecuencia suelen estar en presente simple.

Estructura: If + presente simple, presente simple
If you heat water to 100°C, it boils. (Verdad general)
If it rains, the ground gets wet.

First conditional (posible en el futuro)

Uso: situaciones reales y posibles en el futuro; la condición es probable.

Estructura: If + presente simple, will + verbo
If it rains tomorrow, we will cancel the picnic.
If you study, you will pass the exam.

También se usan otras modalidades en la cláusula principal: can, may, o el imperativo.

Second conditional (hipótesis en el presente / futuro improbable)

Uso: situaciones hipotéticas o improbables ahora o en el futuro.

Estructura: If + pasado simple, would + verbo
If I won the lottery, I would travel the world. (poco probable)
If she had more time, she would learn Italian.

Nota: el pasado simple aquí no indica tiempo pasado real, sino grado de irrealidad.

Third conditional (hipótesis sobre el pasado)

Uso: imaginar resultados diferentes en el pasado — lamentaciones, arrepentimientos, o explicación de resultados pasados.

Estructura: If + past perfect (had + participio), would have + participio
If they had left earlier, they would have arrived on time.
If I had known about the party, I would have gone.

Mixed conditionals

Uso: combinar tiempos cuando la condición en un tiempo produce una consecuencia en otro.

If I had studied harder (past), I would be (present) a doctor now. — pasado → presente
If she were younger (present), she would have applied (past).

Otros usos y variantes

Inversión

En registros más formales se puede invertir la cláusula con had, were, o should sin if:

Had I known, I would have told you. (If I had known...)
Were he here, he would help us. (If he were here...)

Unless

Unless = unless/no... (equivale a if not):

I won't go unless you come. = If you don't come, I won't go.

As if / As though

Para comparar o describir situaciones irreales:

He talks as if he knew everything. (pero no sabe)

Ejercicios

Intenta completar los siguientes ejercicios. Pulsa "Mostrar respuesta" para comprobar.

1) If it _____ (rain) tomorrow, we _____ (cancel) the trip.
If it rains tomorrow, we will cancel the trip. (First conditional)
2) If I _____ (be) you, I _____ (not / do) that.
If I were you, I would not do that. (Second conditional)
3) If she _____ (study) harder, she _____ (pass) the exam last year.
If she had studied harder, she would have passed the exam last year. (Third conditional)
4) Water _____ (boil) if you _____ (heat) it to 100°C.
Water boils if you heat it to 100°C. (Zero conditional)
5) If I _____ (take) that job, I _____ (live) in London now.
If I had taken that job, I would be living in London now. (Mixed conditional: past → present)
6) I won't pass the test unless I _____ (study).
I won't pass the test unless I study. = If I don't study, I won't pass. (Unless = if not)
7) Had I _____ (know) about the traffic, I _____ (leave) earlier.
Had I known about the traffic, I would have left earlier. (Inversion = If I had known...)
8) He speaks as if he _____ (be) the boss.
He speaks as if he were the boss. (As if → unreal present)
